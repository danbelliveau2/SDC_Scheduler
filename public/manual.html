The basics

The SDC Scheduler is our in-house project scheduling app. Each project (a job, like 1160_Y Site Automation) has one schedule: a task grid on the left and a Gantt chart on the right, aligned row-by-row. Everything saves automatically to the server as you edit — there is no Save button. Other people's changes appear live.

  • Tabs across the top are your open schedules. Active projects sit in the top row; open Sales schedules get their own SALES row underneath. + New tab opens an existing schedule; new schedules are created from the Projects page.
  • Grid / Both / Gantt picks the pane layout. Both is the everyday view.
  • Combined ▾ picks what kinds of rows show: Combined (default), Schedule only, or Actions only. Actions are date-anchored to-dos that live alongside scheduled work (they render italic blue).
  • The ⟳ in the tab bar is a hard refresh — use it after being told a new rev shipped.

How a schedule is structured

Every schedule follows the same skeleton, top to bottom:

SectionWhat lives there
05 KICKOFFReceipt of PO, Backlog, information gathering — everything before engineering starts. Collapsible like any section.
10 DESIGN & BUILDMechanical / Controls / General Engineering, Procurement, Build, Wire. Ends at Machine Power-Up.
40 MACHINE TESTINGEngineering + Shop testing. Ends at FAT.
50 TEARDOWN & INSTALLTeardown, Ship Machine, Install (Shop then Engineering). Ends at SAT.

Click any section header to collapse or expand it — the Gantt hides those bars too and automatically re-fits to what's visible. Sections whose tasks are all complete open collapsed automatically, so an in-flight project always opens focused on the remaining work.

Key milestones (anchors)

Six green-chip milestones form the spine of every project: Receipt of PO Mech 1 Release Machine PowerUp FAT Ship Machine SAT. They are always zero-duration milestones (green diamonds with dates on the Gantt) — if one ever arrives from an import with a duration, the app repairs it automatically. Their names work as predecessor shortcuts: type FAT +1w as a predecessor or financial trigger.

SDC conventions

Weeks come in halves. Durations always read 0.5w, 1w, 1.5w… never 0.6 or 1.4. Nothing nonzero reads below 0.5w — a 1-day task shows as 0.5W. A duration of 0 makes the row a milestone (diamond, no bar).
One work-week = 5 business days. The Gantt compresses weekends out — bars and dates are Monday–Friday.

Working in the grid

Editing cells

  • Task name, dates, assignee, comments — click the cell and type.
  • Duration — click the bold value at the right side of the Task column (e.g. 10W) and type 3w, 5d, or 0 (makes it a milestone). Type = then click another row to link durations (🔗 badge) — the linked task always matches its source's duration.
  • Milestones show no duration, but hover the row and a faint —W appears in the same spot — click it and type 2w to turn the milestone back into a task.
  • % complete — click the small pill in the Task column and type a number. Milestones use a checkbox instead (hover the row to see it) — click to mark done. The server stamps the Completed date automatically at 100%.
  • Allocation — the percentage before the task name (e.g. 85%). It's what the person actually spends on this task; hours = duration × allocation × 8/day.

Predecessors

The Pred column drives the schedule cascade — change a task's dates and everything downstream moves. Reference other rows by their line number:

You typeMeaning
12Start after row 12 finishes (FS is the default)
12SSStart when row 12 starts
12FFFinish when row 12 finishes
12SFFinish when row 12 starts
12FS +2w / 12 -3dSame, with a lag or lead
FAT +1w, PO, Ship, SAT, Power-UpAnchor shortcuts — no line number needed
5, 8FF -1wMultiple predecessors, comma-separated

Right-click menu

Right-click any row for:

  • ＋ Add task below / ＋ Add action below — the new row lands directly under the clicked one and inherits its section. (This replaced the old buttons at the bottom of the grid.)
  • ＋ Add additional resource — a second person on the same work.
  • Move to section… — reclassify a row into any section/department, including 05 KICKOFF.
  • Copy to machine — on multi-machine projects.

Right-click a column header to set the grid font size (headers at N, values 1px smaller).

Setting up your columns

  • Show/hide columns ▾ — checkboxes for every column; drag the ⋮⋮ handles to reorder. The Task column can't be hidden, but its four sub-columns are indented under it and toggle independently: Alloc, Description, Duration, % Complete.
  • Resize a column by dragging the edge of its header. Reorder by dragging the header itself.
  • Font size — right-click any column header and pick a size: headers render at that size, all values 1px smaller.
  • ⇤ Compress — one click shrinks every column to exactly its content plus a uniform gap (descriptions never clip), then moves the grid/Gantt divider flush against the last column so the Gantt gets everything you freed. Use it after changing columns.
  • Drag the grid/Gantt divider anytime — the last column stretches to fill, no dead strip.

Views — saving and sharing column setups

A view is a snapshot of your whole column arrangement: which columns show, their order and widths, the Task sub-columns, and the grid font. The Views ▾ dropdown holds three tiers:

  1. Active Project Default / Sales Default — the pinned team standards, same for everybody, locked once set. Opening a Sales schedule applies the Sales Default automatically; switching back to an Active project restores whatever you had.
  2. Shared — views anyone has published, shown with the owner's name. Visible to the whole team.
  3. My views — private to your browser. Nobody else sees them.

To make your own: arrange the columns how you like (hide, reorder, ⇤ Compress), then Views ▾ → + New view from current columns… and name it. To share it: click the ★ next to it — it moves to the Shared section for everyone. Clicking any view applies it, auto-compresses, and zoom-fits; the button label shows which view you're on (and clears when you customize away from it).

Filters

Filters ▾ opens the quick-filter popover. These are show only X filters — nothing is filtered until you check something:

  • Overdue / Behind — only tasks projecting late.
  • Ahead — only tasks running early.
  • Show completed — only finished work (for reviews).
  • Only critical — just the critical-path chain from Receipt of PO.
  • Per-person / per-machine — narrow to one assignee or machine.

The yellow dash on the Filters button means a filter is active. Hide completed items is separate — it lives in the View menu and simply hides done rows rather than filtering to a subset. On Sales schedules all filters and pills start off.

The Gantt chart

The chart mirrors the grid row-for-row. Bars are read-only — drag one and it snaps back; all edits happen in the grid so predecessors can't silently break. Click a bar to jump the grid to that row.

Zoom & fit

  • ⛶ (yellow) — fit the project's visible span to the panel width. Runs automatically when you switch panes, switch projects, or collapse/expand a section.
  • ↕ (yellow) — fit the grid's height to your screen: rows resize so the whole schedule is visible with zero scrolling. Great on laptops.
  • Mouse-wheel zoom — click the chart first to "arm" it (blue outline appears), then scroll to zoom. Click anywhere else to disarm — so scrolling the page never gets hijacked by the chart.
  • Zoom ▾ — stepper for zoom level (1–10) and row height (0–100).

The stats box (top-right of the Gantt)

The floating lime-bordered box summarizes the whole project. It has two modes — the toggle in its top-right corner switches them (your choice sticks):

  • Simple (default) — just the live schedule numbers: total Duration in weeks, labor-weighted % Complete, and the scheduled FAT date.
  • ▸ Advanced — every row becomes a Quoted / Scheduled comparison:
    • Duration — sold delivery weeks vs what the schedule currently adds up to.
    • Complete — where the project should be at today's date vs where it actually is.
    • FAT — the quoted FAT date vs the scheduled one, with a +1W/−1W variance chip (red late, green early).
    • Penalty — appears when the contract has a penalty clause: the date it kicks in (PO + N weeks) and a "past" flag if FAT is scheduled beyond it.

The quoted side comes from the Project Release / sold values. On multi-machine projects a scope line says which machine subset the numbers cover.

Baseline (the ◉ target pill)

A baseline freezes the plan of record: a snapshot of every task's start and finish at the moment you set it. From then on the app can always answer "how far off the original plan are we?" — independent of the day-to-day behind/ahead chips.

Setting one: right-click the ◉ target pill (last one in the toggle block) → Turn on baseline. Today's dates become the plan of record. Typical moment: right after Accept & apply on the Project Release, when the schedule reflects what was sold. If no baseline exists yet, a plain left-click offers to set one.

Viewing it: left-click ◉ to toggle the overlay (pill lights blue when showing). With it on you see:

  • Dashed ghost outlines on the Gantt showing where each bar was originally planned — the gap between ghost and bar is the slip, visually.
  • A ±w chip to the left of every task that moved: −1w finishing earlier than planned, +2.5w later. Based on the finish date.

Where else it shows: the Departments tab's Key Milestones strips show each anchor's baseline date struck through next to the current date, with a slip chip that reads trending ±w (hollow — a forecast, milestone still open) or ✓ ±w (solid — final, actual vs baseline). Hover any chip for all three dates: Baseline → Scheduled → Actual. The grid's Start/Finish also show baseline strike-through dates when they differ.

Maintaining it: right-click ◉ → Reset baseline overwrites the snapshot with today's dates (a re-plan becomes the new plan of record); Turn off baseline clears it entirely. Baselines are per-project.

Baseline chips vs the red/green schedule chips: the baseline chip (left of the bar, only when ◉ is on) compares against the frozen plan. The schedule chip (right of the bar, always on for started work) projects today's % complete forward. A task can be ahead of one and behind the other.

Actions (to-dos)

An action is a date-anchored to-do that lives in the schedule without being scheduled work — "call the customer about paint spec," "send drawings for approval." They render italic blue, default to a zero-duration milestone, and turn red when past due and incomplete.

  • Add one: right-click the row it belongs near → ＋ Add action below. It inherits that row's section. Give it a date (or a predecessor) and optionally an owner.
  • See them: the Combined ▾ toolbar dropdown — Combined shows schedule + actions (default), Actions shows only the to-dos, Schedule hides them.
  • Complete one: hover the row and click the checkbox in the Task column.
  • The Notes sidebar page aggregates actions across all projects, and signed-in users get a personal tab showing just theirs.

Filters ▾ (separate popover) holds the quick "show only X" filters — overdue, ahead, critical path — plus per-person and per-machine narrowing. On Sales schedules all pills default off; your settings come back when you return to an Active schedule.

Export for customer

The Export button builds a clean customer-ready view:

  1. Pick which rows to include (all pre-checked; spine milestones always included).
  2. Pick extra columns — % Complete is on by default; Duration and Assigned To optional.
  3. Leave "Auto-fit row height" checked and hit Generate Export.

The app enters a stripped-down customer view fitted to one screen — press Ctrl+P to save a landscape PDF, then click ✕ Exit customer view to get everything back exactly as it was.

Sales schedules

Sales schedules are pre-quote what-if schedules. They live in the Sales workspace (own tab row, own section on the Projects page) and behave slightly differently:

  • Create from the Projects page → Sales section → + New from template. Name it, pick the source (defaults to the SDC sales template), done. New sales schedules land with Receipt of PO two Mondays out — never in the past.
  • The Sales Default view applies automatically (typically hides Assigned To / % complete — nothing is staffed yet). No baseline, no drift chips — nothing is committed yet.
  • The footer button reads 📊 Estimate instead of Project Release: attach the estimate .xlsx and its summary hours feed the Δ quoted-vs-scheduled overlay per section.
  • When the job is won, use → Project on the Projects page to promote it into a detailed Active schedule.

Project Release

The Project Release is how a won job's contract facts get into the schedule. On Active projects, the footer button is 📄 Project Release. Workflow:

  1. Upload the release — click the button, pick the SDC Project Release (.docx). The app reads it directly: PO receipt date, delivery weeks, the financial milestone percentages, penalty clause, and the budget/hours table (read automatically, including from the budget picture).
  2. Check the fields — every extracted value is editable in the panel. If the parse got something wrong, just fix it in place.
  3. Check the budget grid — laid out like the release itself (ME / CE Design / CE Software / General / Mech Build / Elec Build / Testing / Teardown & Install / Parts Cost). These hours are editable — if a number doesn't match the estimate, type the right one. This grid is what feeds the Δ quoted-vs-scheduled overlay on the schedule, so getting it right makes the per-department comparison meaningful.
  4. Financial milestones — the billing grid (%, Description, Trigger, Date, Sent) is built into the panel. Triggers accept anchor shortcuts (PO, FAT +1w, SAT) or line numbers, so billing dates ride the schedule automatically as it moves. Tick Sent when an invoice goes out — the Departments tab's financial timeline turns that event green.
  5. Accept & apply — one click, no confirmation: sets the PO date (shifting the whole schedule to land on it), the sold delivery weeks, and the penalty clause. This is the moment to set the baseline.

View original re-opens the uploaded document; Replace swaps in a corrected file and re-reads it.

Importing from Smartsheet

From the Projects page → + New from template dialog → "Or import from Smartsheet (.xlsx)". In Smartsheet: Export → Export to Microsoft Excel, then pick that file here.

  1. A review grid opens: ☑ Include | Description | Assigned To | Section.
  2. Every task gets a best-guess section from its name and assignee ("Test Engineer" → 40, "Builder 1" → Build, "ME Placeholder" → Mech Eng). Blanks are outlined amber — fill or leave them (blanks land in 05 KICKOFF).
  3. Uncheck rows you don't want — predecessors automatically chain through skipped rows to the nearest imported task.
  4. Hit Create schedule. Predecessors, lags, forward references, baselines, and % complete all carry over; spine milestones import as proper anchors.

Pick multiple files at once to bulk-import (each becomes its own schedule, no review step).

Multi-machine projects

For jobs building the same machine more than once (M1, M2, M3…). Design work is shared; build/wire/test repeats per machine. Step by step:

  1. Click + Add machine (machine sub-tab strip). If the project has no machines yet, existing work is auto-tagged M1 first.
  2. A yellow clone banner appears. Pick the source machine from the dropdown (M4 can copy M1 — it doesn't have to be the latest) and name the new machine.
  3. Click each row you want duplicated into the new machine — typically the build/wire/test work, not the shared design rows. The count in the banner tracks your picks.
  4. Double-click a selected row to give its copy a custom predecessor — e.g. M2's Build starts when M1's Build finishes: type the M1 line number.
  5. Hit Complete. The copies are created, tagged with the new machine, and wired up.
  • Machine sub-tabs (All / M1 / M2…) filter the whole schedule to one machine; line numbers stay stable across subsets.
  • The M pill color-codes rows and Gantt bars by machine (auto-hides on single-machine projects).
  • Missed a row? Right-click it → Copy to machine.
  • The stats box shows which machine subset its numbers cover.

Projects page & workspaces

  • Three workspaces: Active, Sales, Closed. Assignments are saved server-side, so everyone sees the same grouping. Right-click a project row to change workspace, rename, mark as template ★, or delete.
  • Templates show in blue inside a framed box at the top of their workspace. "+ New from template" clones one — tasks, milestones, predecessors, duration links; real assignees blanked, placeholders kept.
  • Rename migrates everything (tasks, financials, notes, history) with no leftovers. Delete requires admin.
  • ★ favorites and Recents have their own sidebar pages for quick access.

Departments tab

  • Key Milestones — one strip per selected project: the six spine milestones as diamonds (green ✓ done, red ! overdue), each with dates, baseline strike-throughs, and trending/final slip chips. Financial milestones ride the same line as $-circles next to the milestone they bill against, labeled with their %.
  • Financial Milestones timeline — schedule-style view of invoiceable events across projects: month header, dashed Today line, one row per project, diamonds colored by status (red = invoice now, amber = due soon, blue = upcoming, green = paid).
  • Team / capacity — the resource Gantt shows each person's load; the cards below cover behind/unstaffed/overloaded work per discipline. (Wheel zoom here is click-to-arm, same as the schedule chart.)

Notes & Procurement

Notes

The Notes bar at the bottom of every schedule opens the per-project notebook. How to use it:

  • Add a meeting — each dated session collects free-form note lines. Type in the composer and hit Add.
  • ⭐ Star a note to promote it to Key Information on the right — the always-visible facts (voltage, contact names, access rules) that shouldn't be buried in meeting history.
  • Notes can carry an Inform tag (who needs to know) and a due date — the pickers open on a single click.
  • Resize the panel by dragging its top edge. The bar shows the meeting/key counts even while collapsed.

Procurement & Job Hours

  • Procurement (bottom bar) — once the project is linked to its ETO job: part readiness by vendor, PO lines, received counts, and % ready at a glance. The ⚙ Shop Parts and 🚚 Vendor POs sidebar pages aggregate the same data across every job.
  • Job Hours (sidebar) — actual charged hours from the ERP against the scheduled hours, per discipline, when the job link is set.

Connecting a project to ETO

Next to the project name banner are two link chips:

  • 🔗 Link ETO job… — tie this schedule to its Total ETO job number. This is what lights up the Procurement drawer (parts/PO readiness), the ETO actuals strip in the quote comparison, and project costing lookups. Click the chip, pick or type the job number, done — the chip then shows ETO #1162.
  • 🔗 Link PBI job… — tie it to the Power BI hours job so the Job Hours page can show actual charged hours vs the schedule.

Link both when a job goes live — everything downstream (procurement readiness, hours tracking, costing) keys off these two connections. If the Procurement drawer says "No ETO job linked," this is the fix.
